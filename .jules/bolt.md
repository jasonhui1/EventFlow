## 2024-05-26 - O(N*M) lookup bottlenecks in recursive graph algorithms
**Learning:** The `simulateEvent` flow in `simulationUtils.js` suffered from significant performance degradation during recursive pathfinding and graph traversals due to nested array lookups (e.g., `nodes.find` inside `getInheritedPrompts`, and `nodes.filter` inside `processFields`). The bottleneck was exacerbated in large graphs because O(N) filtering ran continuously inside loops.
**Action:** When implementing or optimizing recursive graph traversal functions on the frontend or utility scripts, always precompute O(1) Maps for nodes (`nodesMap`) and incoming/outgoing edges (`outgoingEdgesMap`) at the top level and pass them through the recursion context. Additionally, use a single O(N) pass to pre-sort nodes rather than multiple O(N) filters for disjoint sets.
